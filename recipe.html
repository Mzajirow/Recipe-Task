Nigerian Fried Rice Recipe

Fried rice is a popular Nigerian delicacy that infuses a bit of intercontinental cooking style. It has a rich burst of flavor and is packed with vegetables and beef liver for an authentic taste. It is commonly served at events and in Nigerian homes. This is a simple recipe that can easily be recreated.

Recipe Information

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking time: 20 minutes
  • Number of servings: 5 people
  • Difficulty level: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups parboiled rice
  • ½ cup chicken stock
  • 3 tablespoon oil for frying
  • 1 medium-sized onion (diced)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables carrots, sweet peas, sweet corn and green beans
  • ½ teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 scallions (sliced)
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 1 cup beef liver (cubed)
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice in stock

    2. Add the parboiled rice to the stock in a medium pot and cook on medium heat until the water dries up.

  2. Heat the oil

    3. Preheat oil in a pan on medium-high heat.

  3. Sauté the aromatics and vegetables

    4. Add the onions and fry for 1–2 minutes. Then add scallions, mixed vegetables, thyme, curry powder, salt, and the stock cube.

  4. Add liver and rice

    5. Throw in the beef liver and the cooked rice, then stir-fry for 3–5 minutes on high heat.

  5. Finish and serve

    6. Remove from heat and serve hot.


    Tips for perfect fried rice

    Cook the rice al dente to avoid mushiness, and don't overcook the veggies to keep it crunchy. For that coveted crisp, fry on high heat while stirring continuously.

    Note: This recipe uses beef liver, but you can substitute or add shrimp, another protein, or skip the liver for a vegan option.
